## What is Lido

Lido is the leading liquid staking solution - providing a simple and secure way to earn interest on your digital assets. By staking with Lido your assets remain liquid and can be used across a range of DeFi applications, earning extra yield.
 
1. **Staking pool**. Protocol to manage deposits, staking rewards, and withdrawals. A separate one for every supported network. 
2. `st[token]`. Unlike staked assets, the Lido `st[token]` are freely transferable instead of locked as in the case of native staking. Lido lets users operate with staked assets to gain yield on top of yield by leveraging collateral, lending, yield farming, and other kinds of Defi protocols. 
3. **DAO**. Lido liquid protocols management entity, responsible for picking node operators, configuring the protocol parameters and much more.

## Liquid staking 

### Problem statement

Traditionally, staking in Proof-of-Stake (PoS) protocol based projects has been about locking oneâ€™s assets in one project for a long time and expecting a fixed, predetermined staking reward in return. While it guarantees the return on staked assets much like a bond, it also limits the opportunities of generating higher returns on those assets from the DeFi ecosystem. If youâ€™ve staked all of your crypto holdings, you canâ€™t invest or trade in more profitable crypto pairs on exchanges. 

### Solution 
 
Liquid staking allows using the staked crypto assets in other trading or investing opportunities to let the user get the best of both worlds - a reward on your staked assets, as well as the returns from new trading/investing opportunities. Liquid staking introduce various fundamental benefits by: 
- Allowing users to earn staking rewards without fully locking their `st[token]`;  
- Making it possible to earn rewards on as small a deposit as users want (i.e, Ethereum requires minimum 32 `ETH` staked);  
- Providing the `st[token]` a building block for other applications and protocols (e.g., as collateral in lending or other trading DeFi solutions);  
- Providing an alternative to exchange staking, solo staking, and other semi-custodial and decentralised protocols. 
 
### Advantages over solo staking and exchange staking 
 
**Solo staking** is great, but it comes with some disadvantages. Setting up a validator node requires a pristine technical understanding; brings with it a minimum deposit of 32 ETH; slashing and offline penalties can get very severe if the staking is managed improperly; the staked amount is locked up for a significant period. Eventually, it leads to a small number of stakers, which is bad for any PoS ecosystem. 
 
Alternatively, it may be possible to produce staking through **centralised exchanges**. First of all, crypto assets and CeFi are not suited well together from the fundamental standpoint.. It is also worth mentioning the economic aspect - by staking within an centralised entity, the user does not receive a corresponding token in return and, thus, loses the opportunity to perform any subsequent activity within DeFi or the same centralised entity, where assets were staked. Yes, APR, when staking on centralised exchanges might be higher, but with a significant amount aggregated within the centralised entity comes a huge potential influence to the ecosystem that was fundamentally designed decentralised.  
 
Through the use of a **liquid staking** solution such as Lido, users can eliminate these inconveniences and benefit from secure, non-custodial staking backed by industry leaders.

## Security

Lido is a secure liquid staking solution for a number of reasons:
- Use of DAO for governance decisions & to manage risk factors.
- Presence of multiple audits (see more).
- Committee of elected, best-in-class validators to minimise staking risk.
- Staking across multiple validators (i.e. current Ethereum staking mechanism only allows to choose only a single validator)
- Use of non-custodial staking service to eliminate counterparty risk.

We are constantly working on security improvements. In the [scorecard](https://scorecard.lido.fi/) you may find an  outlined set of attributes that we think are important for the decentralisation of the protocol, and how Lido is faring against these targets.
